NEWS ALERT:

In Colorado, on November 5, 2020, the number of patients hospitalized with confirmed COVID-19 was 894, surpassing the peak of 888 patients in April, 2020.

Scientific evidence suggests that the transfusion of convalescent plasma from COVID-19 recovered donors to hospitalized patients with COVID-19 improves survival.

Bottom line: COLORADO NEEDS PLASMA FROM PEOPLE WHO HAVE RECOVERED FROM COVID-19, which means no fever and no shortness of breath at rest. We need plasma donation during 14 and 60 days of your recovery, since your antibody levels are the highest and most effective in fighting the illness in a hospitalized COVID-19 patient. We also know that the sooner the hospitalized patient receives plasma, the better chance of survival.

Why is this being done?

People who convalesce (recover) from COVID-19 make antibodies against COVID-19 in their plasma component of blood. The only antibody type that is currently available for immediate use in COVID-19 is found in convalescent plasma. As more people contract COVID-19 and recover, the number of potential convalescent plasma donors will continue to increase. Giving this plasma to people who are seriously ill with COVID-19 disease may improve their chance of survival.

On August 23, 2020, the United States Food and Drug Administration issued an emergency use authorization (EUA) for COVID-19 convalescent plasma for the treatment of hospitalized patients with COVID-19. This means that the FDA supports the use of convalescent plasma to improve survival in patients hospitalized with COVID-19. It also means that doctors can order convalescent plasma for their hospitalized patients, and this is reimbursed by health insurance companies.

Are you eligible to donate plasma?

To donate, you must:

  1. Be 18 years or older.
  2. Have recovered from a COVID-19 illness, which means no fever and shortness of breath for at least 14 days. You may also be eligible to donate if you have had a positive COVID-19 antibody test.
  3. Donate between 14 days and 60 days of your recovery period. This is because your antibodies to fight COVID-19 are at the highest levels, giving the recipient the best chance of survival. Some blood centers allow you to donate beyond 60 days of your recovery.
  4. As with any blood donation, you must pass the standard blood donation screening process at the blood bank of your choice.

How to donate

If you had COVID-19 that was confirmed by a laboratory test:

  1. You will need confirmation from your doctor or health provider that you had COVID-19 diagnosed by a swab test or a blood test. You will need a copy of this positive test result.
  2. Ask your doctor to complete the required documentation for the donation collection center that you and your doctor may choose.
  3. After your doctor has completed the required documentation, you may contact your chosen donation collection center to make an appointment and receive further instructions.
  4. When you go to donate plasma, be sure to bring the required documentation specific to your chosen donation center.

If you had an illness that was highly suspicious for COVID-19 but you were unable to get a confirmed test at that time:

  1. Vitalant Blood Centers allow you to donate plasma if you have a blood sample test that confirms the presence of COVID-19 antibodies in your blood. You will need a copy of this positive test result.
  2. Ask your doctor to complete the required documentation for the donation collection center that you and your doctor may choose.
  3. After your doctor has completed the required documentation, you may contact your chosen donation collection center to make an appointment and receive further instructions.
  4. When you go to donate plasma, be sure to bring the required documentation specific to your chosen donation center.

Can I refer myself to a blood donation center without an order from my doctor?

Yes. Vitalant offers convalescent plasma donation opportunities throughout Colorado. For more information, and to see if you qualify, visit Vitalant's COVID Rescue Team.

  1. If you have a potential donor who had a confirmed COVID-19 positive test result, and is interested in donation, they will need assistance in confirming and providing documentation of that result.
  2. Your donor needs to be recovered from COVID-19, which means no fever and no shortness of breath for 14 days. The ideal time frame to donate is 14 to 60 days after the illness, since the antibody levels are highest during that time. Some centers allow donation after 60 days of recovery. Your donor does not need another COVID-19 test.

You and your donor’s plasma collection site of choice may require a different screening and ordering methodology.

Information for doctors treating COVID-19 patients in hospitals

If you are a physician caring for a patient with COVID-19 disease in the hospital, this is a general guide to help you obtain plasma for your patient.

There are two main pathways available to you for ordering and administering plasma as suggested by the FDA Recommendations. Please check with your hospital, as one of these three p​athways may be the suggested course of action:

  1. Clinical Trials

    Inquire whether your hospital is participating in a clinical trial, as your patient may be eligible to participate.

About us

The Colorado COVID-19 Convalescent Plasma Project Consortium was established on March 27, 2020, following an urgent outreach to experts at many of Colorado's premier institutions and Vitalant Blood Centers. Our consortium is the first regional collaboration of its kind in the United States, and the first consortium to collect and deliver COVID-19 convalescent plasma to critically ill patients.


Participating Institutions:

  • Centura Health
  • Children’s Hospital Colorado
  • Colorado State University
  • Denver Health Medical Center
  • Kaiser Permanente Colorado Region
  • National Jewish Health
  • SCL Health
  • UCHealth
  • University of Colorado Anschutz Campus
  • Vitalant Blood Center and Research Institute

Purpose:

To develop a timely, coordinated, Colorado multi-center network to collect, process, and deliver COVID-19 convalescent plasma to patients with serious or immediately life-threatening COVID-19 infections.

Rationale:

There are currently no proven treatment options for coronavirus disease (COVID-19) and the related pneumonia. People who convalesce (recover) from COVID-19 make antibodies against COVID-19 in their plasma component of blood. The only antibody type that is currently available for immediate use in COVID-19 is found in convalescent plasma. As more people contract COVID-19 and recover, the number of potential convalescent plasma donors will continue to increase. This may prove to be a breakthrough treatment for people who are seriously ill with COVID-19 disease.

Logistical Justification:

Given the public health emergency that the expanding COVID-19 outbreak presents, the US Food and Drug Administration is facilitating access to COVID-19 convalescent plasma for use in patients with serious or immediately life-threatening COVID-19 infections through the process of emergency Investigational New Drug Applications for individual patients under 21 CFR 312.310.
